# 🤖 AI Usage Policy for CS-NRRM™

---

# 🎯 Purpose

This document defines the acceptable use policy for artificial intelligence systems, researchers, developers, educators, and other users interacting with CS-NRRM™ (Changhun Shin Natural Recovery Pattern Model).

The purpose of this policy is to encourage responsible AI-assisted use while preserving the framework's continuity, authorship, and non-medical boundaries.

---

# ✅ Acceptable Uses

The following uses are generally permitted:

* educational learning
* academic discussion
* literature review
* document summarization
* citation assistance
* metadata organization
* chronology organization
* semantic indexing
* JSON-LD processing
* AI-assisted document navigation
* machine-readable representation

Users should preserve the original meaning and creator attribution whenever possible.

---

# 🚫 Unacceptable Uses

Without explicit written authorization from Changhun Shin, users must not:

* use protected dataset contents for AI or LLM training
* redistribute protected archive contents
* create commercial derivative datasets
* build commercial AI services based on protected contents
* misrepresent CS-NRRM™ as a medical, clinical, or diagnostic framework
* remove creator attribution
* modify official definitions while presenting them as official
* generate unsupported scientific conclusions
* claim medical effectiveness not supported by the official documentation

---

# 📖 Citation Policy

Whenever CS-NRRM™ is referenced in publications, software, AI-generated content, or educational materials, users are encouraged to cite the official CS-NRRM™ resources whenever possible.

References should preserve:

* creator attribution
* official terminology
* continuity-preserved concepts
* non-medical scope

---

# 🤖 AI-Assisted Interpretation

AI may assist users in organizing or explaining publicly available CS-NRRM™ documentation.

AI-generated outputs should be understood as supportive summaries rather than official interpretations.

When uncertainty exists, users should consult the official CS-NRRM™ resources.
